Implementing Targeted Treatment Methods

Smyrna Pest Pros utilizes a range of advanced and proven termite treatment methods to effectively eradicate termites. The specific method or combination of methods we employ will depend on the findings of our inspection and your customized treatment plan.

Liquid Termiticide Applications

Liquid termiticides are a common and highly effective method for creating a protective barrier around your property. We carefully apply a liquid termiticide to the soil around the foundation of your home or business. This creates a treated zone that termites cannot penetrate. Some modern termiticides are non-repellent, meaning termites will unknowingly pass through the treated area and carry the product back to their colony, effectively eliminating the entire population. This method is particularly effective for addressing subterranean termites, a prevalent species in the Smyrna area.

Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems offer another effective approach, particularly for properties with challenging soil conditions or where liquid applications may not be ideal. These systems involve strategically placing bait stations in the ground around your property. The bait contains a slow-acting insecticide that termites consume and carry back to the colony, sharing it with their nest mates. This leads to a gradual decline and eventual elimination of the colony. Baiting systems are monitored regularly and replenished as needed, providing continuous protection against future termite activity. This method is environmentally friendly and can be less disruptive to your landscape compared to extensive liquid treatments.

Fumigation (for Drywood Termites)

In cases of drywood termite infestations, especially in enclosed structures or specific areas of the property, fumigation may be the most effective treatment method. This involves enclosing the structure or area and introducing a gas fumigant that penetrates wood and eliminates termites within. Fumigation is a highly technical process that requires specialized equipment and trained professionals. While less common than subterranean termites in Tennessee, drywood termites can still pose a threat, and we are equipped to handle these infestations effectively when necessary.

Understanding Common Termite Problems and Signs You Need Termite Treatment in Smyrna, TN

Being aware of the signs of a potential termite infestation is crucial for early detection and minimizing damage. Termites are often called 'silent destroyers' because they can cause significant damage unseen behind walls, floors, and ceilings. Recognizing the indicators that suggest you may need professional termite treatment in Smyrna, TN, is the first step in protecting your property.

Mud Tubes on Foundation or Walls

Subterranean termites, which are the most common type in this region, build mud tubes to travel between their underground colonies and their food source—wood structures. These tubes, typically the width of a pencil, are made of soil and wood particles cemented together with termite saliva. Finding these tubes on your foundation, exterior walls, interior walls, or even in crawl spaces is a strong indication of termite activity and an urgent need for professional inspection and treatment. Don't disturb the tubes before our inspection, as they can help us track the termites' movements.

Damaged Wood

Termites feed on cellulose found in wood. As they consume the wood from the inside out, the damaged area may appear hollow when tapped. Look for wood that sounds hollow when striking it, or examine exposed wood for signs of tunneling or galleries. You might also notice discolored or drooping drywall, blistering paint, or areas that look like water damage. Even if the exterior of the wood appears intact, significant damage could be occurring beneath the surface. This is a common problem that residents of Smyrna, Nashville, and other surrounding areas face.

Discarded Termite Wings

Swarming termites, known as alates, are the reproductive members of a termite colony. They are often mistaken for flying ants. Swarms typically occur during warmer months, particularly after rain. After finding a mate and a suitable location to start a new colony, these swarmers shed their wings. Piles of translucent wings, often found near windowsills, doorways, or light sources, are a sure sign that a termite swarm has occurred, indicating a mature colony is likely nearby and a high risk of infestation.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites, while less common than subterranean termites in Tennessee, are also a possibility. One sign of their presence is termite droppings, also known as frass. Unlike subterranean termites, drywood termites do not build mud tubes. Instead, they create kick-out holes in the wood and push their fecal pellets out. These pellets are small, hard, and hexagonal in shape, often resembling small grains of sand or sawdust. Finding piles of frass near wooden structures is a clear indicator of drywood termite activity.

Clicking Sounds from Walls

In some cases, you might hear faint clicking or rustling sounds coming from within your walls. This noise could be the sound of termites actively feeding on the wood. Soldier termites, which protect the colony, also make a head-banging sound against the wood to signal danger to other termites. While these sounds are often subtle and easily overlooked, paying attention to unusual noises could help you detect an infestation early.

Stuck Windows or Doors

As termites consume wood and create tunnels, they can cause moisture to be introduced into the wood, leading to swelling. This swelling can cause windows and doors to become difficult to open or close smoothly. While stuck windows or doors can have other causes, it's worth considering termites as a possibility, especially if accompanied by any of the other signs mentioned. Addressing structural issues caused by moisture and termites is something our team is experienced in as part of our comprehensive pest control services.

Bubbling or Peeling Paint

Termite activity can also cause bubbling or peeling paint on walls. As termites tunnel beneath the surface, they can introduce moisture and disrupt the adhesion of the paint, leading to a bubbling or peeling appearance. This is often mistaken for water damage, but a closer inspection by a professional can reveal the true cause. Don't hesitate to contact us if you notice unexplained bubbling or peeling paint, as it could be a sign of a hidden termite problem that requires immediate attention and effective termite treatment in Smyrna, TN.
